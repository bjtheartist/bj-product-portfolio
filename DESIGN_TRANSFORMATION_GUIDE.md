# TemsVision Website Aesthetic Transformation Guide

## From Current Design → Neobrutalist + Lorenzo-Inspired Hero

This guide explains how to transform your portfolio website aesthetic to mirror the Lorenzo Dal Dosso style while adding neobrutalist elements with floating objects and depth. Written for non-coders to understand conceptually.

---

## Part 1: Understanding the Design Languages

### What is Neobrutalism?

Think of neobrutalism as **"raw meets playful."** It's a design style that combines:

| Element | Description | Visual Example |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Bold Borders** | Thick, black outlines around elements (like comic book style) | Boxes with 2-4px black borders |
| **Flat Colors** | Bright, solid colors without gradients | Yellow, pink, blue blocks |
| **Hard Shadows** | Shadows that look like offset duplicates, not soft blurs | A box with a solid black shadow shifted 4px down and right |
| **Playful Shapes** | Geometric shapes floating around | Circles, stars, squiggles |
| **Bold Typography** | Chunky, heavy fonts that demand attention | Extra-bold sans-serif fonts |

### What Makes Lorenzo Dal Dosso's Site Special?

Lorenzo's site is **minimal brutalism** - it takes brutalist ideas but keeps them elegant:

1. **Cream Background** - Warm off-white (`#F5F5DC` or `#FAF9F6`) instead of pure white
2. **Custom Typography** - The "o" letters become diamond shapes (◊) - a signature branding element
3. **Horizontal Sections** - Clear horizontal bands dividing content
4. **Progress Indicator** - "01 ——— 04" showing where you are on the page
5. **Scrolling Marquee** - Services scroll horizontally at the bottom
6. **Minimal Navigation** - Just "Menu" and "Contact" in corners

## Part 3: The Transformation - Conceptual Breakdown

### Layer 1: The Background Canvas

**What to Change:**
- Remove the dark/light theme toggle (commit to one aesthetic)
- Set background to warm cream color
- Remove the grid pattern overlay
- Remove rotating hero background images

**Why:**
Lorenzo's site feels calm and sophisticated because of the warm, neutral background. It lets the typography and floating elements be the stars.

**Color to Use:** `#FAF9F6` (warm cream) or `#F5F5DC` (beige)

---

### Layer 2: Your Name as the Hero

**What to Change:**
- Replace the logo image with large typographic name
- Make "TemsVision" or "Temilade" the dominant visual element
- Consider a custom character treatment (like Lorenzo's diamond "o")

**Concept:**
```
Current:  [Logo Image]
          [Small tagline in brackets]

Target:   T E M S V I S I O N
          (massive, bold, spans the width)
          
          or
          
          Temilade
          Amire Quadri
          (stacked, with one word emphasized)
```

**Typography Ideas:**
- Use a bold display font (keep Bebas Neue or try: Archivo Black, Oswald, or a custom variable font)
- Make the name 80-120px on desktop, 40-60px on mobile
- Consider replacing a letter with a symbol (like ◊ or ●) for branding

---

### Layer 3: Floating Objects (The "Bouncing" Elements)

**What to Add:**
Floating elements that move slightly as you scroll or on a gentle animation loop.

**Ideas for Your Photography Brand:**

| Object | Description | Animation |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Camera Icon** | Simple line drawing of a camera | Gentle float up/down |
| **Photo Frames** | Small tilted rectangles (like polaroids) | Slow rotation |
| **Geometric Shapes** | Circles, triangles in your brand colors | Bounce/pulse |
| **Your Photos** | Small circular or diamond-shaped photo cutouts | Parallax movement |
| **Stars/Sparkles** | Small decorative elements | Twinkle animation |

**Depth Concept:**
Imagine 3 invisible layers:
1. **Back Layer** - Large, faded shapes (move slow when scrolling)
2. **Middle Layer** - Your main content (name, text)
3. **Front Layer** - Small floating objects (move faster when scrolling)

This creates the "depth" feeling you mentioned.

## Part 6: Animation Concepts

### Floating Animation (CSS Concept)

```
Imagine an element that:
1. Starts at position Y
2. Moves up 10 pixels over 2 seconds
3. Moves back down 10 pixels over 2 seconds
4. Repeats forever

This creates a gentle "floating" effect.
```

### Parallax Depth (Scroll Concept)

```
When you scroll down 100 pixels:
- Back layer elements move up 20 pixels (slow)
- Middle layer moves up 100 pixels (normal)
- Front layer elements move up 150 pixels (fast)

This creates the illusion of depth, like looking through a window.
```

### Marquee Animation (Scroll Concept)

```
Text starts off-screen to the right
Text moves left continuously
When text exits left side, it reappears on the right
Creates infinite scrolling effect
```

## Part 8: Questions to Decide

Before implementation, decide:

1. **Name Display:** "TemsVision" or "Temilade Amire Quadri" or both?
2. **Custom Character:** Want to replace a letter with a symbol (like Lorenzo's ◊)?
3. **Floating Photos:** Include small photos of your work, or keep it abstract shapes only?
